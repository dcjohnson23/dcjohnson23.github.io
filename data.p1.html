Data management

My publications have been analysed and summarised in R, Python and BASH with data from clinical trials databases and our in-house data server build on SQL. Sequence analysis pipelines that I have utilised have been built and calling through python. I have been updating my old scripts to encompass newer techniques, including utilising TCGA and DREAM projects leaders approaches. I have also been translating that to python more scalable machine and deep learning approaches on my Github, that is currently private as contains patient data.

LIMS experience included using Progeny LIMS for sample processing and bio-banking. I have also used LIMS together with liquid handlers automation including Hamilton (NGS Star) and Qiagen (QIAglity) in conjunction with high-throughput assays.

I'm experienced at dynamic documents, version control, containerization & workflow engines, I now work exclusively in JupterNotebook environments. In my last position I worked on three informatic platforms/levels: On a desktop, with lots of (64GB) RAM running Rstudio and Ananconda; I maintained a local linux server for testing scripts and housing shiny apps; utilised ICR Scientific Computing Infrastructure which included a 2,000 core, 2PB scratch High Performance Computing system, a 6.5 petabytes storage and a 4x NVIDIA Tesla P100 GPU array. Over the last few months I have come to appreciate with huge sized sets that moving forward that most work will be carried out in Docker containers and with interfaces such as Colab, Azure and Kaggle.

I have extensive experience in analysing public genomic, transcriptomic and proteomics datasets, including:

- NCBI GEO Datasets and EMBL-EBI ArrayExpress, through which I’ve regularly accessed .CEL Affymetrix U133+2 RNA arrays and SNP data for eQTL analysis, prognostic signature work and hypotheses generating boxplots and prognostic curves (typically quantile or tertile), to reinforce molecular biology work within the lab, comparing preclinical experimental paired profiles, from knockdown/upregulated, drug-treated pairs, using Limma/Deseq2 packages.

- NCBI GEO Datasets where I also accessed Cancer Cell Line Encyclopaedia (CCLE) data, which I used to examine characteristics of drug sensitive by IC50 to examine to show similar patterns to which we showed in primary experiments.

- European Genome-phenome Archive (EGA) for methylation data from blueprint consortium which I for replication and annotation;

- Wash Epi-genome Browser for Chromatin Interaction Analysis by Paired-End Tag Sequencing ChIA-PET and ENCODE RoadMap browser again for annotation i.e. Linc-RNA.

- MMRF Commpass study which is also part of TCGA and can be accessed through the GDC Data portal - International Myeloma consortium - To evaluate neutral cancer status on overall and progression free survival: I utilised whole exon data, correcting variant allele frequencies (VAF) with copy number generated from low pass whole genome sequencing. In a recent DNA methylation biomarker project, I utilised pair-end RNA sequencing data (FPKM) in conjunction with FISH-seq data to carry out quartile cox proportional with age, sex, subgroup to replicate DNA methylation findings.
